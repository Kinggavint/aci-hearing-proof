Cannabis Use and Hearing Health

Numerous states currently allow the utilization of marijuana, THC, or cannabinoid materials for medicinal perposes, and many states have legalized marijuana for recreational purposes.

So, what are cannabinoids?

Cannabinoids are categorized as a group of compounds found in the cannabis or marijuana plant.

Cannabinoids are sometimes believed to possess extensive healing qualities, but established research suggests there might also be unfavorable effects like a strong connection between cannabinoid usage and the progression of hearing loss or tinnitus symptoms.

How many types of cannabinoids are there?

At present, cannabinoids can be used in lots of forms.

Beyond its many nicknames, like weed, ganja, or reefer, marijuana has broader significance that exceeds its nicknames.

Cannabinoids are now available in forms including oils, sprays, capsules, and more.

Depending on the state, and according to federal laws, different types of cannabinoids will be available, while numerous varieties remain restricted if their THC concentration exceeds 0.3%.

So it’s still normal for people to be very cautious about cannabinoids.

The issue is that we don’t presently understand some of the lasting side effects or consequences of cannabinoid use.

For example, new findings reveal how cannabinoids can impact your hearing ability.

Cannabinoids and hearing loss

Cannabinoids are thought to be helpful for a wide range of health problems and medical conditions.

Seizures, nausea, vertigo, and more seem to be helped by cannabinoids, according to some research.

Researchers then investigated whether cannabinoids might also provide relief for people suffering from tinnitus.

Continuing studies indicate that using cannabis could have an impact on your auditory health.

  • High Blood Pressure: Cannabis use can possibly result in increased blood pressure as one of its side effects.

Prolonged elevated blood pressure has the potential to harm blood vessels in various regions of the body, including those within the inner ear. As a result, this damage can lead to hearing loss and the feeling of ringing in the ears known as tinnitus.

  • Ototoxicity: Some of the chemicals in cannabis might be ototoxic, meaning they are toxic to the inner ear. This can also result in hearing loss and tinnitus.

Using cannabinoids can indeed result in the progression of tinnitus.

Based on the research, over 20% of study participants who used cannabinoid products reported hearing a ringing in their ears.

Prior to the study, the participants did not experience any indications of tinnitus. Furthermore, people who used marijuana were considerably more prone to reporting tinnitus symptoms within 24 hours.

Additional studies have suggested that using marijuana might exacerbate the ringing experience associated with tinnitus in people who already cope with this condition.

Simply put, research indicates that tinnitus and cannabinoids may not be a great combination.

The possible negative impact of cannabinoids on tinnitus symptoms

Cannabinoids can worsen your tinnitus in a few specific ways.

First off, the incidents of tinnitus symptoms can become more regular, you may notice the buzzing or ringing in your ears more frequently.

Additionally, using cannabinoids might intensify your tinnitus episodes, leading to louder and more persistent ringing that can be more difficult to ignore.

The beginning of tinnitus symptoms has also been connected to cannabinoids.

Basically, beginning to use cannabinoids might result in the inception of tinnitus symptoms, even if you didn’t experience them previously.

The underlying cause of tinnitus remains uncertain

Even though we acknowledge the connection between tinnitus and particular triggers, we have yet to establish the exact governing reasons.

Cannabinoids may influence the middle ear and tinnitus symptoms, but it’s still uncertain why.

Clearly, research is continuing.

As we acquire a better sense of how cannabinoids are linked to tinnitus, people will have the knowledge necessary to make an educated decision on which type of cannabinoid to pick.

caution:

Cannabinoids have garnered significant recognition and publicity in the market over the last several years.

A major factor is the shifting public viewpoint concerning cannabinoids, which also demonstrates an increasing interest in looking into alternatives to opioids.

However, new research has illuminated the potential downsides of cannabinoids, particularly for people who value their hearing health, revealing that these compounds can have adverse effects on hearing.

Tinnitus and cannabinoids are clearly connected based on existing research.

So regardless of how much marketing you see for CBD oils, if you’re worried about tinnitus, you should probably avoid them.

With the solid connection between cannabinoids and tinnitus, it’s a smart plan to use caution.

Don’t hesitate to get a professional assessment if you’re dealing with tinnitus or hearing troubles – prioritizing your auditory wellness is essential for your overall health.
