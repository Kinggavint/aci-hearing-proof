Find the Right Hearing Aid for Your Life

At ACI Hearing Center, we believe that no two patients are the same. That is why we carry hearing aids from a wide range of top manufacturers, and we take the time to match you with the technology that fits your hearing loss, lifestyle, and budget.

Our Fitting Process

  1. Comprehensive hearing evaluation — We start with a full hearing test to understand the type and degree of your hearing loss.
  2. Lifestyle consultation — We discuss your daily activities, communication challenges, and goals for better hearing.
  3. Device selection — We recommend hearing aids based on your test results, preferences, and budget.
  4. Custom programming — Your hearing aids are programmed specifically for your hearing profile.
  5. Real-ear measurement verification — We verify the fit using probe microphone measurements to ensure accuracy.
  6. Follow-up care — We schedule follow-up appointments to fine-tune your devices and ensure satisfaction.

Real-Ear Measurements: The Gold Standard

Real-ear measurement (also called probe microphone verification) is considered the gold standard in hearing aid fitting. A tiny microphone is placed in your ear canal alongside your hearing aid to measure exactly what sound your ear is receiving.

This process ensures your hearing aids are delivering the precise amplification your ears need — not just a "best guess" based on software. Research shows that hearing aids verified with real-ear measurements provide significantly better speech understanding.

Only about 30% of hearing aid providers perform real-ear measurements. At ACI Hearing Center, we do it on every single fitting. It is part of our commitment to best practices and your best hearing outcome.

See our full real-ear measurement page for the 6-step process, pediatric and cochlear-implant use cases, and how to check whether your current hearing aids were verified this way.

Before we fit a hearing aid, we also measure how well you understand speech in noise using validated tests like QuickSIN. Your SNR loss score tells us exactly how much noise management your hearing aid needs, and how to set the directional microphone and noise reduction features.
