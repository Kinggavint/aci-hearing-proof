ReSound Nexia Hearing Aids in Lafayette, LA

ACI Hearing Center fits the full ReSound lineup as an independent practice in Lafayette, Louisiana. We do not have a manufacturer contract with ReSound or any other brand. We recommend ReSound Nexia when the audiogram, ear anatomy, and lifestyle add up to it. This page explains what makes Nexia different, which patients benefit most from it, and what to expect from a ReSound fit at ACI.

Why ReSound Nexia leads for public streaming

ReSound is a Danish hearing aid brand owned by GN Store Nord, one of the six largest manufacturers in the world. What sets Nexia apart is that ReSound was the first company to ship a hearing aid with full support for Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast reception. This matters because Auracast is the emerging public-broadcast standard that lets any venue send audio directly to nearby Bluetooth LE Audio devices. Think about how helpful that is when a flight delay announcement, church sermon, or gym class instruction is otherwise hard to hear over background noise.

Auracast is rolling out in stages across the country. Louis Armstrong International Airport in New Orleans (MSY) has piloted Bluetooth broadcast for gate announcements. Churches with hearing loops are starting to add Auracast broadcast alongside legacy loops. Universities and conference centers are next. Over the next 2-3 years, patients with Auracast-ready hearing aids will notice they can just "tune in" to public announcements without struggling to hear over PA distortion.

Nexia is not the only brand adding LE Audio support. Phonak, Oticon, and others are catching up. But Nexia shipped first and has the most mature implementation as of 2026. If public streaming is your top priority, Nexia is the current pick.

The trade-off: Nexia is not the most aggressive on noise suppression in loud restaurants. That crown belongs to Phonak Audeo Sphere Infinio. If restaurants and noisy family dinners are your biggest complaint, we will steer you to Phonak instead. If your biggest complaint is airports, church PA systems, gym classes, or large conference rooms, Nexia is our pick.

Current ReSound models we fit

ReSound Nexia (adult, first-line)

Nexia is a receiver-in-canal device and our most-fit ReSound model. Rechargeable-only, about 24 hours per charge without streaming, 20 hours with streaming. That is a longer battery life than most competitors. Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast reception built in, plus full iPhone (MFi) and Android (ASHA) compatibility. Built-in T-coil for legacy hearing loops. Motion tracking through the ReSound Smart 3D app.

What we like: Nexia's Auracast leadership is real. It is not a spec-sheet feature yet. Patients who spend time at large venues, in transit, or in houses of worship notice the difference in the first month. What to know: Nexia's noise-in-crowd performance is balanced, not aggressive. It preserves natural sound scene rather than aggressively stripping background noise. Patients whose top complaint is restaurants often prefer Phonak.

ReSound OMNIA (previous generation, still supported)

OMNIA was the ReSound flagship before Nexia and remains available for existing OMNIA patients who need replacements or upgrades. OMNIA is a solid balanced hearing aid but does not have full Auracast reception. We do not first-fit OMNIA anymore. If you already wear OMNIA and are happy, we service them. If you are new to ReSound, we go straight to Nexia.

ReSound ENZO Q (severe to profound, super-power BTE)

ENZO Q is a super-power behind-the-ear device for adults with severe to profound loss. Available with disposable battery for maximum output. Compatible with the ReSound Multi Mic remote-microphone system for one-on-one conversations. For patients on the edge of cochlear implant candidacy, ENZO Q is one of the three super-power devices we consider alongside Phonak Naida and Oticon Xceed.

How a ReSound fit works at ACI

  1. Full audiological workup. Air conduction, bone conduction, speech-in-noise, tympanometry, and if indicated, otoacoustic emissions. This tells us your loss shape and how much room there is for improvement with amplification.
  2. Discussion and recommendation. We walk you through the top 2-3 brands that fit your audiogram, lifestyle, and budget. ReSound Nexia is often on that list, especially if you spend meaningful time in public streaming environments. If a different brand fits better we say so.
  3. Prescriptive fit with real-ear measurement. We do not just plug in the manufacturer's first-fit software. We use a probe microphone in your ear canal to measure the actual output at your eardrum and match it to a validated prescription (NAL-NL2 for adults). Only about 30% of dispensers actually do this. Every ACI fit gets REM verification.
  4. 45-day trial and follow-up fine-tuning. You wear the devices in your real life. We fine-tune based on what you notice. Included in the fee.
  5. 3-year manufacturer warranty and 3-year loss-and-damage coverage on premium tiers.

Frequently asked questions about ReSound at ACI

Does ACI Hearing Center fit ReSound hearing aids in Lafayette, LA?

Yes. ReSound is one of the six major hearing aid brands we fit as an independent practice. We fit the full current lineup: Nexia (adult first-line), OMNIA (still-supported previous generation for existing patients), and ENZO Q for severe-to-profound loss. All fits are verified with real-ear measurement.

What is Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast, and why does ReSound Nexia lead on it?

Bluetooth LE Audio is the next-generation Bluetooth standard for hearing aids and earbuds. Auracast is the public-broadcast layer that lets any venue (airports, houses of worship, gyms, conference rooms) broadcast audio to nearby Bluetooth LE Audio devices. ReSound Nexia was the first hearing aid on the market to ship with full Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast reception. As Auracast rolls out across Louisiana over the next 2-3 years, Nexia patients will be first in line to benefit.

How does ReSound Nexia compare to Phonak Audeo Sphere Infinio?

Both are premium receiver-in-canal hearing aids. Phonak leads on aggressive AI noise suppression for very noisy environments and hands-free phone calls. ReSound Nexia leads on Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast public streaming. Patients whose main frustration is restaurants and noisy family dinners usually prefer Phonak. Patients whose main frustration is streaming from airports, church PA systems, gym classes, or large venues usually prefer ReSound. We help you pick based on your actual daily life.

Does ReSound Nexia work with iPhone and Android?

Yes. Nexia works with iPhone via Apple's Made for iPhone protocol and with Android via Android ASHA. It also supports Bluetooth LE Audio streaming for direct audio from newer smartphones and tablets. Nexia was designed for cross-platform streaming from the start.

Are ReSound Nexia hearing aids rechargeable?

Yes. ReSound Nexia is rechargeable-only and gives about 24 hours per charge without streaming, or about 20 hours with streaming. That is a longer battery life than most competitors. OMNIA is available with rechargeable or disposable battery. ENZO Q is available with disposable battery.

How much do ReSound Nexia hearing aids cost at ACI Hearing Center?

At ACI Hearing Center, we price complete treatment plans, not just the ReSound device. Treatment plans that include ReSound Nexia, OMNIA, or ENZO Q binaural hearing aids range from $4,500 to $7,500 and include a real-ear measurement fitting, a 45-day trial, follow-up adjustments, and a 3-year manufacturer warranty. Every plan qualifies for 0% financing through Cherry, CareCredit, or PowerPay for qualified applicants.

Does ReSound Nexia work with hearing loops and Auracast broadcast at churches or airports?

Yes to both. Nexia has a built-in T-coil so it picks up traditional hearing loops installed at churches, theaters, and airports (Lafayette Regional Airport does not currently have a public hearing loop, but MSY New Orleans does). More importantly, Nexia receives Auracast broadcast directly, and venues in Louisiana are starting to add Auracast alongside or instead of legacy loops.

Does ReSound Nexia support fall detection or health tracking?

Nexia does not have built-in fall detection. Starkey Genesis AI leads on that feature. Nexia does have basic motion tracking through the ReSound Smart 3D app. If fall detection or heart-rate monitoring is important, we can walk you through why Starkey may be a better pick.
