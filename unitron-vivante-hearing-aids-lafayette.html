Why patients pick Unitron over other brands

Unitron sits inside Sonova, the same parent company that owns Phonak. That means Unitron shares the parent chip platform. What Unitron does with it is different. Unitron tunes the sound to feel natural out of the box, keeps the patient-facing app simple, and is the only brand we fit that offers a Flex trial. Flex lets you take a higher technology tier home for a defined trial window in your real life. If it is worth the difference to you, you keep the higher tier. If it is not, we step you down and you pay for the lower tier. That solves the single hardest question in hearing aid buying: is the top-tier chip actually worth the money for the way I live?

The second reason patients pick Unitron: Log It All. It tracks quietly in the background how much of your week you spend in each type of listening environment, and we use that log at the follow-up visit to fine-tune programming to your real life. Two patients with identical audiograms will end up with very different Unitron programming after their first log review. That is why the follow-up matters more with Unitron than with most brands.

Unitron Vivante models we fit

Moxi V (flagship RIC)

The mainline behind-the-ear rechargeable receiver-in-canal device on the Vivante platform. Full access to the Unitron sound classifier, Log It All environment tracking, and Bluetooth LE Audio streaming. About 20 hours per charge. Fits mild to severe hearing loss. This is the Unitron we fit most often.

Stride V (power BTE)

A behind-the-ear device with a stronger receiver for severe to profound hearing loss, or for patients who need a more durable device that can be handled easily by a caregiver. Same Vivante features. The physical build is designed to be dropped, cleaned, and handled without damaging small parts.

Insera V (custom in-the-ear)

Custom fit in-the-ear rechargeable made from a scan of your ear at ACI Hearing Center. Sits in the outer bowl of the ear. Good compromise for patients who cannot tolerate anything behind the ear but also do not need a completely-in-canal fit. Rechargeable, and includes streaming.

How the Unitron Flex trial works, step by step

The reason we go to Unitron for a lot of first-time users is Flex. Here is how a Flex fitting plays out at ACI Hearing Center:

  1. We do the hearing test and a real-ear measurement fitting on a Unitron Vivante device programmed at a mid-range technology tier.
  2. During your trial window we bump the same device up to a higher technology tier at no extra cost, so you can experience the top-tier chip in your real life.
  3. You wear both tiers in the environments that matter to you: church, restaurants, small group meetings, at home with grandkids, driving.
  4. At the follow-up visit we look at the Log It All data plus your own feedback. If the higher tier is clearly worth the price difference, we set the device to that tier and you pay for it. If it is not, we step you down and you only pay for the lower tier.

You get to decide with real information instead of guessing in the fitting room. That is why the Flex trial is the number-one reason we bring Unitron into the conversation for value-tier patients.

Common questions about Unitron Vivante

What makes Unitron different from Phonak if they are owned by the same company?

Unitron and Phonak both sit inside Sonova, and they share the parent chip platform, but Unitron tunes the sound processing, the app, and the fitting philosophy for a more accessible, value-oriented user experience. Phonak leads with the newest features at the top tier. Unitron leads with the Flex trial program, a simpler patient app, and a strong story on how the sound feels natural out of the box. If you are budget-conscious and want a real second opinion in the Sonova family, Unitron is the play.

What is the Unitron Flex trial and why does it matter?

Flex is a Unitron feature that lets you try a higher technology level for a defined trial window in your real life. If you find the higher tier is worth the difference, you keep it. If not, we step you down and you pay for the lower tier. No other major manufacturer offers this. It solves the biggest problem in hearing aid buying: you cannot really tell in a fitting room whether the top-tier chip is worth the money for the way you live. Flex lets your own week decide.

What Unitron models does ACI Hearing Center fit?

Three main styles on the Vivante platform: Moxi V (behind-the-ear rechargeable receiver-in-canal, the flagship shape), Stride V (behind-the-ear for stronger power needs), and Insera V (custom in-the-ear rechargeable made from a scan of your ear). We match the model to your ear shape, hearing loss, and lifestyle at the fitting.

What does a Unitron treatment plan cost at ACI Hearing Center?

Our treatment plans at ACI Hearing Center range from $4,500 to $7,500 and include binaural hearing aids (both ears), a real-ear measurement fitting, follow-up adjustments, cleanings, and our care commitment for the life of your devices. Where you land in that range depends on the technology level and lifestyle needs, not the brand you pick. Unitron is often a strong value-tier choice inside this plan because of the Flex trial program.

Does Unitron work with an iPhone?

Yes. Unitron Vivante streams directly from iPhone using Made for iPhone, and from modern Android phones using Bluetooth LE Audio and Auracast. The Remote Plus app on your phone gives you volume, program switching, and access to a simpler control layout than most competitors.

What is Log It All and how do you use it?

Log It All is a Unitron feature that quietly tracks how much time you spend in each type of sound environment: quiet, small groups, noisy restaurants, music, streaming, and so on. At your follow-up visit we look at the log and fine-tune your programming to match your real life, not what we guessed at the initial fitting. Patients who use hearing aids in unusual environments (musicians, church leaders, kids who play sports) get the most out of this feature.

Are Unitron hearing aids a good fit for a first-time user?

Yes. Unitron is one of the brands we recommend most often for first-time users who are price-sensitive or feature-cautious. The Flex trial takes the pressure off of picking the exact right technology tier on Day 1. The Remote Plus app is simpler than the flagship apps from Phonak or Oticon. And the sound tuning is designed to feel natural in the first two weeks, which is the window in which most drop-outs happen.

How long does the battery last on a charge?

The Moxi V rechargeable runs about 20 hours per charge with typical use. Streaming and Bluetooth-heavy days will pull that down. The charger stores extra charges for travel.
